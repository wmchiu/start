Michael Phelps

The Gold Medal Specialist

2000-2016

2000 Sydney Olympics

At the age of ten, Phelps decided to train under Bob
Bowman, the head coach of the North Baltimore Aquatic
Club. He would soon break national age-group records and
to this day, still holds twelve of them. Due to his rapid success,
Phelps qualified for the Olympics at the age of 15 as the youngest
male in 68 years. He would unfortunately not medal but still
obtained fifth place.

2004 Athens Olympics

In winning six gold and two bronze
medals, Phelps, still a teenager, had the
second-best performance ever at a single
Olympics, behind Mark Spitz's seven gold
medals at the 1972 Summer Olympics.

2008 Beijing Olympics

Phelps, upon completing the event that awarded him his eighth
gold medal and eighth Olympic record in as many events,
"Records are always made to be broken no matter what they are ...
Anybody can do anything that they set their mind to."

2012 London Olympics

Phelps became the first male swimmer
to win the 100m butterfly and 200m individual medley
titles at three consecutive Games. As well as landing
the 4x200m freestyle relay title, he took silver in the
200m butterfly and the 4x100m freestyle relay before
claiming an 18th Olympic gold in the 4x100m medley.

2016 Rio Olympics
















“Being able to close the door on this sport how I wanted to –
that’s why I’m happy now,” Phelps said after completing
his six-medal haul in Rio. “I was a little kid with a dream,
which turned into a couple of medals. Just being able to
finish this way is special because now I’m able to
start the next chapter in my life.”
